Biography

I am looking for PhD students, especially those interested in glacier - ocean interactions. Please contact me for more information.

I am an Assistant Professor in Physical Oceanography at Oregon State University. I am interested in ocean physics over length scales from turbulence (millimeters) to mesoscale eddies (hundreds of kilometers). Questions that motivate me include: “how do currents influence ice melt at glaicers?”, “how is mixing generated when currents hit topography?”, and “how much energy is exchanged between eddies and internal waves?”. My approach combines high resolution in situ observations with numerical simulations. I practice open science by making my analysis and software available online.
